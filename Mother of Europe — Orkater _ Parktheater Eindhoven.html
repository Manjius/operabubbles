On the eve of the French Revolution, tensions are brewing in Europe. The powerful Empress Maria Theresa, the talented young violinist and composer Chevalier, and Freemason Angelo Soliman meet at a crucial moment in time, with only one goal: to leave an indelible mark on European history.

Mother of Europe  is a story about the fickleness of power, about the struggle to be part of the status quo and to allow all voices to be heard in historical narratives. With a blend of classical strings, explosive percussion, and electronic sounds, the characters sing themselves free from time and place.

About the makers:
Belle van Heerikhuizen is a director and has been involved with Orkater since 2019 with her collective KONVOOI, through the talent development program Orkater/De Nieuwkomers. With this program, she created successful productions such as ILIAS , De Meester , and Margarita , and recently performed High-Rise . Van Heerikhuizen is also involved with Orkater as a director outside of the collective. In 2020, she created Alles Komt Goed (Everything Comes Well) , and in 2023, she set the Amsterdamse Bostheater ablaze with Wildfire , which received critical acclaim. 

Manoushka Zeegelaar Breeveld has been a regular creator at Orkater since 2018. She recently completed the successful Suriname trilogy, in collaboration with the Bijlmer Parktheater ( Woiski vs. Woiski , De Gliphoeve , Stepping Stones ). She also appeared in the summer spectacle Het Verdriet van de Zuiderzee , by Orkater and De Lawei, and in Alles Komt Goed (Everything Comes Well ). In 2024, she initiated the production De plantage van onze familieren (The Plantation of Our Ancestors) at Orkater, based on the podcast of the same name. The performance was a great success and sold out.

Yariv Vroom first became involved with Orkater as a composer in 2024. He created the compositions and music for the successful production "The Plantation of Our Ancestors ."
